InSight · privacy

Privacy policy

last updated 2026-05-17

InSight is a private journal. Your data is yours. This page is the long version of what that means in practice — what we collect, why, who else can see it, and how to get rid of it.

What we collect, why

Everything below is stored under your account on Firebase (Google Cloud), in the project's configured region. We don't sell, rent, or share your data with advertisers — there are no advertisers. Each row says what, why, and who else can see it by default.

What we don't collect

  • Phone numbers, mailing addresses, or government IDs.
  • Contacts list from your phone.
  • Browsing history outside the app.
  • Microphone, accelerometer, or any sensor we haven't asked for.
  • Any data for advertising. We don't sell ads.
  • Any data for cross-app tracking. We're not part of an advertising network.

Anonymous aggregate data

When you opt in to being discoverable and have taken the Big Five (and optionally politics or values) test, your scores contribute to per-area averages alongside everyone else's. Those averages are rolled up by a scheduled server-side job into a single record per ~5 km cell, and only published when the cell has at least 20 contributors. Individual scores cannot be extracted from these averages.

Each test category has its own opt-out in the "what you share" overlay. Setting any of them to "nobody" excludes your scores from the next rebuild.

Sharing with friends

InSight has one cross-user social feature: the daily-report feed. When you add someone as a relation and they have an InSight account, you grant them read access to your daily reports — but only if your "daily report" share level is set to circle or wider. The recipient sees only the fields you ticked when you wrote the report.

A second cross-user surface is "anonymous impressions" — people in your circle can leave a traits-only impression for you. These show up anonymously in your "of you" inbox. The sender's identity is recorded alongside for abuse prevention (so we can rate-limit floods), but the UI never reveals it.

Third parties we use

Firebase (Google Cloud)

Authentication, Firestore for data storage, Cloud Functions for scheduled rollups and account deletion. Subject to Firebase's privacy policy.

Open-Meteo

Weather, sunrise/sunset, and air quality data on the around tab. We send latitude/longitude rounded to two decimals (≈ 1 km) so the API can return localised weather. No identifiers are sent. The response is cached on your device for an hour. Subject to Open-Meteo's free-use terms.

On-device language model (Gemma)

The "what we noticed" reflections in the journal tabs and the age-facts in the life overlay are generated by a small language model that runs entirely on your device. Nothing leaves the phone. The model is downloaded once (~2 GB) and runs offline thereafter.

How long we keep your data

Indefinitely, until you delete your account. Account deletion is immediate and irreversible — see the "danger zone" at the bottom of your portrait.

When you delete your account, we wipe:

  • Every doc under your user subtree (journal, ledger, relations, etc.).
  • Your discoverable-location marker.
  • Any anonymous impressions you've sent to other users.
  • Any relation entries other users have that point at your uid.
  • The Firebase Auth account itself.

Anonymous contributions to area aggregates can't be pulled back out individually — that would defeat the anonymity. The next scheduled rollup rebuilds without you and the cell numbers shift accordingly.

Your rights

  • Access: everything you've written is visible to you inside the app. We don't keep a hidden mirror.
  • Correction: every entry has an edit affordance.
  • Deletion: every entry has a delete button, and the whole account can be deleted from your portrait.
  • Portability: a "download my data" path is on the roadmap; until it ships, contact support for an export.
  • Withdraw consent: turn off "discoverable" in sharing, set any sharePrefs row to "nobody", or delete your account.

Children

InSight isn't designed for children under 13 (or under the minimum age of digital consent in your jurisdiction, whichever is higher). We don't knowingly create accounts for them. If you believe a child has signed up, contact us and we'll delete the account.

Security

  • All traffic to Firebase is HTTPS.
  • Firestore rules enforce that no one but you can read your subtree, except where you've explicitly granted access (daily-report friend feed; inbound impressions from circle).
  • Cross-user writes (only inbound impressions) require the recipient to have already added the sender as a relation.
  • The Cloud Function for account deletion uses admin SDK and runs only when invoked by the authenticated account being deleted.

No system is perfectly secure. If you discover a vulnerability, please contact us directly rather than disclosing publicly so we can fix it.

Changes to this policy

When we change this policy in any material way, we'll bump the "last updated" date at the top and surface a notice in the app. Continued use of InSight after a material change means you accept the new policy.

Contact

Questions, deletion requests, security disclosures — contact us at the support address listed on the app's terms of service page.
